\section{Problem 1.}

$(a, b, c)$ is a solution to the system of equations {$5x + 3y + z = 2$   and  $6x + 5y + 4z = 8$}.  Solve for $a + b + c$.

\begin{solution}
Since we have more parameters than equations, we will have infinite solutions that 
are dependent on the other parameters. We can simply use linear algebra and obtain
our solutions and sum them all together.

\begin{align*}
    & 5x + 3y + z = 2 \\
    & 6x + 5y + 4z = 8 \\
    & \begin{bmatrix}
            5 & 3 & 1 & 2 \\
            6 & 5 & 4 & 8
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
            5 & 3\\
            6 & 5
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 0 & -1 & 2 \\
            0 & 1 & 2 & 4
    \end{bmatrix} \\
    \Longrightarrow & RREF = \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 0 & -1 & 2 \\
            0 & 1 & 2 & 4 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}

Another way to think of this problem is to take the row subspace and see if it includes
the range $[1, 1, 1]$ and to find the linear combination of the rows to obtain that. For simplicity,
let's just solve for $x, y, \text{and }z$ and sum them all up.

\begin{align*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 0 & -1 & 2 \\
            0 & 1 & 2 & 4 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0
    \end{bmatrix} \\
    & \begin{bmatrix}
            x \\
            y \\
            z 
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
            -2 \\
            4 \\
            0 
    \end{bmatrix} 
    +
    t
    \begin{bmatrix}
            1 \\
            -2 \\
            1 
    \end{bmatrix}, \ t \in \mathbb{R} \\
    & x = z - 2, \ y = 4 - 2z, \ z=z \\
    & x + y + z = \left(z-2\right) + \left(4 - 2z\right) + \left(z\right) \\
    \Longrightarrow & \boxed{x + y + z = 2}
\end{align*}


\end{solution}
